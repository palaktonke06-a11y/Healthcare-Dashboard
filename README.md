📊 Project Name: Healthcare Operational & Financial Analytics Dashboard

📝 Project Overview
This project addresses the challenge of managing fragmented clinical, demographic, and financial data in a hospital setting. The objective was to create a centralized solution to monitor operational metrics, patient distributions, and financial health. By designing this interactive dashboard, I provided a solution that allows hospital administrators to track comprehensive billing trends over time, monitor room allocations based on medical criteria like blood types, evaluate patient demographics, and optimize resource distribution efficiently.

🚀 Key Features & Insights
Financial Trend Analysis: Monitors long-term billing patterns and peaks across a multi-year timeline (2020–2024).
Demographic Segmentation: Provides a clear breakdown of patient metrics by gender and age categories.
Resource & Operational Metrics: Analyzes room numbers, specific blood types, and doctor allocation counts to help balance staff workload and hospital capacity.

Visuals Breakdown:
KPI Cards: High-level executive metrics showing a Sum of Billing Amount of 1.42bn, Sum of Age at 3M, and Sum of Room Numbers at 17M.
Pie Chart (Sum of Room Number by Blood Type): Displays an even distribution of room metrics across distinct blood types (A-, A+, B-, B+, O-, AB-), each holding roughly 12% of the allocation share.
Donut Chart (Count of Gender by Gender): Visualizes the patient gender split, tracking the distribution between Male and Female segments.
Line Chart (Sum of Billing Amount by Timeline): A granular, continuous line graph tracking financial billing fluctuations daily, monthly, and quarterly across the years 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024.
Clustered Bar Chart (Count of Doctor and Name by Blood Type & Room Number): Compares the volume of doctors alongside patient names across specific hospital room numbers (ranging from 101 to 126) filtered by blood type.
Data Matrix Slicer: An interactive table tracking granular time-series hierarchies (Year, Quarter, Month, Day) from 2019 through 2020 to slice the entire dashboard dynamically.

🛠️ Tech Stack Used
Tool: Power BI Desktop
Data Source: Healthcare Hospital Management Dataset (Excel / CSV)
DAX Functions & Metrics: Sum of Billing Amount, Count of Gender, Sum of Room Number, and Time Intelligence aggregations.
